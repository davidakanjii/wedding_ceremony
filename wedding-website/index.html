Our Moment

💚 My Husband and I's Story - Taiwo's View

I met David during my NYSC program in Benin in 2018, and he just struck me as a quiet and influential person who could help me with my CDS attendance and clearance, not knowing life had something else in store for both of us. We remained platonic friends throughout our NYSC program. When the NYSC program finished, we went our separate ways, with him checking up on me once in a while via social media.

Fast forward to 2022, David reached out to me and asked for my location. We found out we were in the same city, and then came the pursuit for that one date. He was always checking up on me from time to time and asking for that one date, but I, on the other hand, was always busy and, in between, playing hard to get. Lol.

Then in 2023, I had just gotten my heartbroken 6 months prior, and I woke up one morning and decided to go for this date. Let this man let me rest. We agreed on the day and location. I was extremely late, only because my village people were helping me find all the reasons not to go for that date, but he waited patiently for me.

After two extra hours, I arrived at the location, and when I saw him, my life changed forever. I was captivated immediately — man, he is sooo fine. Like, my husband is the most handsome man I have ever seen. Immediately, I changed my walking step and added more steeze as I was wearing a green dress and black heels. As for me, no bad now.

Then he got talking about what he needed in a woman, and he mentioned the genotype test. Funny enough, I had never been tested — never! We decided to go get tested together the next weekend. Our results were out, and guess what? We were compatible and could actually live together happily ever after. And that's exactly what we are about to do, three years after.

💛 David's View of Taiwo

Honestly, I noticed Mathinda before we even spoke. There was just something about her during NYSC — the way she carried herself, calm and unbothered, like she had already figured out things the rest of us were still working on. I didn't say much at the time. I just paid attention.

When we eventually started talking, it was easy. No awkwardness, no trying too hard. It just flowed. And the more we talked, the more I realised this was not just somebody I enjoyed talking to — this was somebody I wanted to keep around.

Three years later, I can say with full confidence, she is everything I hoped she would be and more. She loves deeply and makes me want to show up better every single day. Choosing her has been one of the easiest decisions of my life, and I look forward to choosing her again and again, for the rest of my life.
